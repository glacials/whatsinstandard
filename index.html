What is Standard (MTG)?

Standard is a rotating group of Magic: The Gathering sets. Most sets enter Standard when they're released and drop out about eighteen months later.

A Standard card is a card printed or reprinted into a set currently in Standard.

A deck which contains only Standard cards is called a Standard deck.

Rotation patterns

The set rotation pattern for Standard is currently in a two-year transition between the old set release pattern and the new one.

How the changes touch Standard

  • Sets enter Standard at the same rate (four per year).
  • Sets rotate out of Standard twice as often, in groups half the size.
  • Therefore the mean number of Standard sets over a rotation period has dropped by one.

Getting through the two-year transition

The Theros block and 2015 Core Set were the last group to rotate out in the old pattern.

The Khans of Tarkir block and Magic Origins, although released in the old pattern, are dropping in the new: Khans of Tarkir and Fate Reforged dropped together in April as if they were a two-set block; Dragons of Tarkir and Magic Origins will drop in the same fashion Q4 2016.

After that, all sets will be both released and dropped in the new style.

What is Standard (Hearthstone)?

Standard format or Standard is a new game format for hearthstone coming in spring 2016. Games played in Standard format include only cards from card sets released in the current or previous calendar year, as well as the Basic and Classic sets. However, effects which generate specific cards will continue to function normally regardless of the generated card's card set.

A Standard format is the counterpart to Wild format, which has no such restrictions.

A deck which contains only Standard cards is called a Standard deck.

The Hearthstone Zodiac

Each Standard year aka Hearthstone year will be symbolised by a constellation formed from stars in Azeroth's night sky. The previous year will rotate out in favor of a new one. Each year will be marked by a different "mythical beast". The current year's constellation will act as a symbol for Standard format, as seen on the Standard format selection button on the Play mode screen.

The Year of the Kraken

With the removal of many cards key to aggressive play, and the introduction with Whispers of the Old Gods of many cards effective at slowing and defending against attackers, the start of the Year of the Kraken is widely considered to herald a move away from the aggressive decks considered by many to have dominated Hearthstone for too long, and toward a slower meta.
